ὁπλότερος, α, ον, Comp. without any Posit. in use, Ep. for νεώτερος, the younger, always of persons, Il.4.325, Od.21.370, B.10.71; ὁπλότερος γενεῇ younger by birth, Il.2.707, Od.19.184: fem. gen. pl. ὁπλοτεράων Il.14.267,275: Sup., youngest, ὁπλότατος γενεῆφιν 9.58; ὁ. θυγάτηρ Od.7.58, cf. 11.283, Hes.Th.946, Pi.I.6(5).6, al.: Ar.Pax1270-1 uses the Comp. in mock heroic lines.—The orig. sense was perh. capable of bearing arms; and so ὁπλότεροι would be the serviceable men, hence, the young men, opp. the old men and children, Il.3.108, Hom.Epigr.4.5; then simply, younger or youngest, hence of women, Νέστορος ὁπλοτάτη θυγάτηρ Od.3.465, etc.; then, as the youngest are the last born, ἄνδρες ὁπλότεροι also means the latter generations, men of later days, Theoc.16.46; ὁπλοτέρου τ’ Ἀχιλῆος ἀκούομεν Εὐρυλόχοιο Euph.80.

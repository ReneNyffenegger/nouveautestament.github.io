πολυπράγμ-ων, ον, gen. ονος, (πρᾶγμα) busy about many things, mostly in bad sense, meddlesome, officious, a busybody, Eup.222, Ar.Av.471, Lys.24.24, Isoc.15.98, 230, 237: freq. as epith. of the restless Athenians, as in the plays so entitled by Timocles, Diphilus, and Heniochus; applied to Socrates, Arr.Epict.3.1.21. Adv. πολυπραγμό-νως Mitteis Chr.31 viii 30 (ii B.C.).     2. later and rarely in good sense, curious after knowledge, π. καὶ περιττός Plb.9.1.4; Ἡρόδοτος ὁ π. D.S.1.37, cf. Antig.Mir.24.

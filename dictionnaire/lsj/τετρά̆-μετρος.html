τετρά̆-μετρος, ον, consisting of four metres, i.e., in iambic and trochaic verse, consisting of four double feet or syzygies: τὸ τετράμετρον is generally the trochaic tetrameter, Ar.Nu.642,645, X.Smp.6.3, Arist.Rh.1404a31, 1409a1, Po.1459b37: also the anapaestic tetrameter, called τὸ Ἀριστοφάνειον (as in Nu.957 sq.), D.H.Comp.25; cf. τρίμετρος.     2. γωνίαι τ. square, i.e. right, angles, Callix.2 (dub. l.).

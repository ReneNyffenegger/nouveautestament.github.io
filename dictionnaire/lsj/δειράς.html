δειράς (Cret. δηράς GDI5024.19), άδος, ἡ, ridge of a chain of hills, h.Ap.281, S.Aj.697 (lyr.), Limen.22; of the isthmus of Corinth, Pi.O.8.52, I.1.10; of Trachis, S.Ph.491: in pl., E.Ph.206 (lyr.): metaph., τέγγει δ’ ὑπ’ ὀφρύσι δειράδας, of the petrified form of Niobe on Mt. Sipylus, which poured tears under the brow of the hill over its ridges, S.Ant.832 (lyr.). (δερσ-, cf. Skt. dr̥s̥ad- 'rock'.)

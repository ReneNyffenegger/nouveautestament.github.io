κάπρος [ᾰ by nature], ὁ, boar, esp. wild boar, Il.17.725, Pl.La.196e, etc.; also σῦς κ. Il.5.783, 17.21, cf. Ar.Lys.202 (ubi v. Sch.); ἧπαρ κάπρου Id.Fr.318.5: in fem. sense, sow, ὀχευομένους τοὺς κάπρους Anaxandr.47.     II. a sea-fish, Capros aper, Philem.79.21, Arist.HA505a13; a species found in the Achelous, ib.535b18. (Cogn. with Lat. caper, ONorse hafr 'he- goat', but not with Lat. aper.)

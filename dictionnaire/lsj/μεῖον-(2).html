μεῖον (B), ου, τό, lamb or sheep offered at the Athenian Apaturia by a father who was enrolling his son among his φράτερες, τοῦ μείου IG 22.1237.5; τὰ μεῖα ib.60; παρέστησε μεῖον Is.Fr.124: expld. by Sch. Ar.Ra.810 (cf. Eratosth. and Apollod. ap. Harp.) as neut. of μείων, too light! from the cry of the φράτερες when the lamb was weighed; cf. μειαγωγέω.

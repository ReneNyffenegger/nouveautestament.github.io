πτερο-φόρᾱς, ου, ὀ, a name of certain sacred officers in Egypt, so called from the hawk's wing worn on their heads, nom. pl. -φόραι OGI56.4 (Canopus, iii B.C.), 90.7 (Rosetta, ii B.C.): also πτερο-φόροι, Hsch.; cf. sq. III, and πτεραφόρος.     II. dat. sg. -φόρᾳ χιλιάρχῳ, perh. name of a military rank, or = πτεροφόρος II, Men.Pk.104, cf. Hsch. s.v. πτεροφόροι.

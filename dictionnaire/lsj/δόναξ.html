δόναξ, ᾰκος, ὁ, poet. gen. δούνακος AP (v. infr.), dat. δώνακι Theoc. (v. infr.): (δονέω, 'a reed shaken with the wind'):—pole-reed, Arundo Donax, smaller than the κάλαμος (Eust.Il.1165.23), Il.10.467, Od.14.474, Thphr.HP4.11.11, etc.; δόνακες καλάμοιο reed-stalks, h.Merc.47.     2. bed of reeds, App.BC3.67, al.     II. anything made of reed,     1. shaft of an arrow, Il.11.584.     2. shepherd's pipe, Pi.P.12.25 (pl.), A.Pr.574 (lyr.), Theoc.20.29.     3. fishing-rod or limed reed, AP7.702 (Apollonid.).     4. bridge of the lyre, Ar.Ra.232.     III. shell-fish, = σωλήν, Diph.Siph. ap. Ath.3.90d.

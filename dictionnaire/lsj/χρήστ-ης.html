χρήστ-ης (written χρείστης SIG364.40 (Ephes., iii B. C.)), ου, ὁ: gen. pl. χρήστων (not χρηστῶν, to distinguish it from the gen. pl. of χρηστός, Hdn.Gr.1.425): (χράω (B) A):—one who gives or expounds oracles, prophet, soothsayer, Hsch., prob. in Milet.7p.50 (Didyma, i B. C.).     II. (κίχρημι) creditor, usurer, Ps.-Phoc.83, Ar.Nu.240, 434 (anap.), Lys.32.29, Lycurg.22, etc.     2. (χράομαι, κίχραμαι) debtor, Phoc.16, D.30.12, 32.12, IPE12.32B84 (Olbia, iii B. C.), cf. Harp. s. v.: c. gen., κακοῦ ἀνδρός Phoc. l.c.; χρημάτων D.36.6.

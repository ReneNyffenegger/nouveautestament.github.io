ἐπίορκ-ος, ον, sworn falsely, of oaths, εἰ δέ τι τῶνδ’ ἐπίορκον Il.19.264: freq. in the phrase ἐπίορκον ὀμόσσαι take a false oath, swear falsely, 3.279,19.260, Hes.Op.282, Th.232; in full, ἐπίορκον ὅρκον ὤμοσε Ar.Ra.150; and so ἐ. ἐπομνύναι (v. ἐπόμνυμι); but in Il.10.332 ἐ. ἐπώμοσε he swore a bootless oath, i.e. one which he meant to fulfil, but the gods willed otherwise.     II. of persons, forsworn, perjured, Hes.Op.804, Schwyzer179a (Crete), E.El.1355 (anap.), Ar.Nu.399,al.: Sup.-ότατος Antipho6.48.

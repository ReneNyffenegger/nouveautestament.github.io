Κᾰρύαι [ῠ], ῶν, αἱ, Caryae, a place in Laconia with a famous temple of Artemis, Th.5.55, etc.:—hence Κᾰρῠᾶτις, ιδος, ἡ, μέλισσα (prob. priestess of Artemis) St.Byz.; as Subst. esp.     1. Artemis, Paus.3.10.7.     2. dance in honour of Artemis, Poll.4.104; cf. καρυατίζω 2.     II. Κᾰρῠάτῐδες, ων, αἱ, priestesses of Artemis at Caryae, Pratin.Lyr.4.     2. Archit., female figures used as bearing-shafts, Lync. ap. Ath.6.241e, Vitr.1.1.5.     3. a kind of ear-rings, Poll.5.97.

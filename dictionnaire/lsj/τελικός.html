τελικός, ή, όν, pertaining to the supreme end, ἀγαθὰ τ. (opp. ποιητικά) Stoic.3.25, al.; κεφάλαια τ. topics drawn from these goods, Hermog.Prog.6, al.     2. more generally, connected with final or intentional causality, τ. αἴτιον, = Aristotle's οὗ ἕνεκα, Procl.in Prm.p.612 S., cf. in Ti.3.126 D.; in Medicine, Alex.Aphr.Febr.23.     II. in the end or termination of a word, Clearch.63, D.T.632.9, Heph.1.2, EM289.33.     III. perfect, εἰς τελικὴν φιλίας ἕξιν prob.in Phld.Acad.Ind.p.56 M.

νεοσσός, Att. νεοττ-ός, ὁ, (νέος) young bird, nestling, chick, Il.2.311, 9.323, S.Ant.425, Ar.Av.835, Ev.Luc.2.24, etc.; ἀπτῆνες ν. Plu.2.48a.     2. any young animal, as a young crocodile, Hdt.2.68; of young children, A.Ch.256, 501, E.Alc.403 (lyr.), al., Pl.Lg.776a: fem., ἦν νεοττὸς καὶ νέα (sc. Lais) Epicr.3.15: in pl., young bees, X.Oec.7.34, Arist.HA624a22; Ἄρεως ν., of the cock, Ar.Av.835 (also ironically, of a person, Pl.Com.104): collective, ἵππου ν. the horse's brood, A.Ag.825.     3. yolk of an egg, Arist.HA565a3, Orac. ap. Chrysipp.Stoic.2.344; cf. νεοττίον.—The disyll. form νοσσός is cited in AB109 from A.Fr.113 and occurs in S.Oxy.2081(b)Fr.3: this and cogn. forms (commonly found in later Gr.) are condemned as ἀδόκιμα by Phryn.182.

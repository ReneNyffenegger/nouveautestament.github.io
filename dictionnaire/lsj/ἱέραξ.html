ἱέραξ [ῐ], ᾱκος, ὁ, Ion. and Ep. ἴρηξ [ῑ], ηκος (the longer form first in Alcm.28, E.Andr.1141, Ps.-Orac. ap. Ar.Eq.1052):—hawk, falcon, ἴρηξ ὠκύπτερος Il.13.62, cf. 819, Od.13.86, Hes.Op.212, Hdt.2.65, Arist.HA620a17; sacred to Apollo, Ar.Av.516.     II. a kind of fish, Epich.68 (in Dor. form ἱάραξ), Epaenet. ap. Ath.7.329a.     III. name for a grade of initiates in Mithras-worship, Porph.Abst.4.16.     IV. name of a bandage, Sor.Fasc.12.

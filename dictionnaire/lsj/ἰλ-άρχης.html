ἰλ-άρχης (later written εἰλ-άρχης, cf. βειλάρχας), ου, ὁ, (ἴλη) commander of a troop of horse, PPetr.3p.8 (iii B.C.), al., Ascl.Tact.7.2, Plu.Tim.31 (pl.), Arr.An.2.7.3; = Lat. praefectus turmae, Plb.6.25.1, 6.35.8.     II. commander of eight elephants, Ascl.Tact.9, Ael.Tact.23:— hence ἰλ-αρχέω, Boeot. ϝιλαρχίω, command cavalry, IG7.3087 (Lebad.), 3206 (Orchom.), 2466 (Thebes).     II. at Rome, to be sevir equitum, D.C.55.10.

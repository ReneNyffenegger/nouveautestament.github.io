ὀπωρ-ινός, ή, όν, of ὀπώρα or late summer, ἀστέρ’ ὀπωρινῷ ἐναλίγκιον, i. e. Sirius, the star whose rising marked the beginning of that season (v. ὀπώρα), Il.5.5; ἦμαρ 16.385; βορέης 21.346, Od.5.328; ὄμβρος Hes.Op.674, 677; ὄρχατοι E.Fr.896; δέλφαξ Ar.Fr.506.4; πυλαία SIG239C31, al. (Delph., iv B. C.). [In Hom. the last syll. is always long (by position in Il.21.346), and the penult. is long also, metri gr.: when the ult. is short, the penult. also is short, as in Hes.Op.674; in Att. ῐ always ; cf. μετοπωρινός.]

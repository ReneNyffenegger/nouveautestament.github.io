ἰήϊος, α, ον, also ος, ον (v. infr. II), epith. of Apollo, the god invoked with the cry ἰή or ἰὴ παιών (v. ἰή), ἰήϊε παιάν Pi.Pae.2.35, cf. A.Ag.146, S.OT154 (both lyr.), 1096, Ar.V.874, A.R.2.702, Duris79J.     II. mournful, grievous, ἰήϊοι κάματοι S.OT174 (lyr.); ἰήϊος βοά, γόος, a cry of mourning, E.Ph.1036, El.1211 (both lyr.). (From the cry ἰή, as Εὔιος from εὐοἵ; but also associated with ἰάομαι, Hsch. (hence applied to Asclepius, IG3.171); and, as ἱήϊος, with ἵημι, Hsch., Macr.Sat.1.17.16.)

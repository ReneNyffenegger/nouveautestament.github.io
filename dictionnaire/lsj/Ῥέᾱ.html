Ῥέᾱ, ἡ, Ep. Ῥείη Il.14.203 (in gen. Ῥείης, v.l. Ῥείας), h.Cer.442, h.Ap.93 (Ῥέη codd.), h.Ven.43, Hes.Th.625 (Ῥείαν ib.135); Ῥεῖα δ’ ὑποδμηθεῖσα ib.453 (but with v.l. Ῥείη δ’ αὖ δμηθεῖσα); gen. Ῥείας AP6.219.20 (Antip.); the common form Ῥέα is found in Il.15.187, as a monosyll.; contr. Ῥῆ Pherecyd.Syr.9; Ῥέη h.Cer.459, Hes.Th.467:—Rhea, daughter of Uranos and Gaia, wife of Cronos, mother of Zeus and the gods, ll. cc. [Derived by Chrysipp.Stoic.2.318 from ῥέω, because rivers flow from Earth.]     II. Pythag. name for 2, Anatolius ap.Theol.Ar.12; also for 23 or 8, ib.55.

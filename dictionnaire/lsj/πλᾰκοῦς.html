πλᾰκοῦς, οῦντος, ὁ, voc. πλακοῦ Theodos.Can.p.3 H.:—contr. from πλακόεις, flat cake (perh. shaped like the mallow-seed, Phan.Hist.29), freq. in Com., πλακοῦντος κύκλος Ar.Ach.1125, cf. Alex.22 (pl., hex.); π. ἄρτος Ath.14.645d: also resolved πλακόεις, AP6.155 (Theodorid.).     II. the seed of the mallow, which seeds children call cheeses, Phan.Hist.29, Gal.10.113.

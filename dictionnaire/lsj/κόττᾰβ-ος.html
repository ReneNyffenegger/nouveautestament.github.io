κόττᾰβ-ος, ὁ, Ion.and older Att. κόσσᾰβος (A.Fr.179.4 (pl.), E.Fr.631 (pl.)), the cottabos, a Sicilian game (Anacr.53, Critias2.1 D.), of throwing heel- taps into a metal basin, described by Ath.15.665d sqq., Sch.Ar.Pax342, 1243, Poll.6.109, Suid. s.v. κοτταβίζειν: κ. is found in various senses,     1. the game itself, Anacr. l.c., Critiasl.c., Pl.Com.69.4, etc.     2. the prize, = κοτταβεῖον 2, Eup.86, cf. Ath.15.667d.     3. the wine thrown, = λάταξ, E. l.c., Antiph.55.5.     4. the basin, = κοτταβεῖον I, Cratin.116, Eup. l.c., Antiph.55.12. (κότταβος ἀσπίδων is prob. f.l. for κόναβος Anon.Rh.3.210S.)

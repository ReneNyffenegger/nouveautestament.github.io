Ἥρα, Ion. Ἥρη, ἡ, Hera, Il.16.432, etc.; νὴ τὴν Ἥραν, an oath of Athen. women, X.Mem.1.5.5.     2. applied to the Empresses of Rome, as Ζεύς to the Emperors, IG9(2).333 (Thess.), CIG3956b (add.).     3. Pythag. name for nine, Theol.Ar.58.     4. the planet Venus, Arist.Mu.392a28, Ti.Locr.96e. (Perh. connected with ἥρως and Lat. servo, cf. Ἡρϝαῷοι, = inhabitants of Heraea.)

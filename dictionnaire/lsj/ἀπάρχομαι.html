ἀπάρχομαι, make a beginning, esp. in sacrifice; τρίχας ἀπάρχεσθαι begin the sacrifice with the hair, i.e. by cutting off the hair from the forehead and throwing it into the fire, κάπρου ἀπὸ τρίχας ἀρξάμενος Il.19.254; ἀλλ’ ὅ γ’ ἀπαρχόμενος τρίχας ἐν πυρὶ βάλλεν Od.14.422: abs., 3.446.     II. later c. gen., cut off part of a thing, offer it, τοῦ ὠτὸς τοῦ κτήνεος Hdt.4.188; ἀ. κόμης E.El.91; τῶν κρεῶν καὶ σπλάγχνων offer part of them, Hdt.4.61: hence,     2. offer the first-lings or first-fruits, πάντων of all sacrifices, Id.3.24: abs., begin a sacrifice, Ar.Ach.244, Pax1056, etc.; ἀ. τοῖς θεοῖς X.Hier.4.2; ἀπηργμένοι, of eunuchs, having had their first- fruits offered, Anaxandr.39.11.     3. metaph., take as the first-fruits, take as the choice or best, δικαστήν Pl.Lg.767c: abs., offer first-fruits, Theoc.17.109.     III. generally, offer, dedicate, χρυσᾶς (sc. δραχμάς) IG2.652B19, cf. Plu.Sull.27, AP7.406 (Theodorid.).     IV. later, = ἄρχομαι, begin, c. gen., πημάτων Lyc.1409: c. inf., v.l. in Luc.Nigr.3; practise, prelude on, ὀργάνων Him.Or.17.2.

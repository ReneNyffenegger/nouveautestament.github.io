Ἴτων [ῐ], ὁ St.Byz., and Ἴτωνος, ὁ, Str.9.5.8, a town in Thessaly: —hence Ἰτωνία, ἡ, title of Athena who was worshipped there, Hecat.2J., Paus.1.13.3, 10.1.10; χρυσαιγίδος Ἰτωνίας ναός B.Fr.11.2; also at Coronea, Plb.4.25.2,25.3.2, Str.9.2.29, Paus.9.33.1; at Athens, IG 12.310.217; at Amorgos, ib.12(7).33 (ii B.C.):—also Ἰτωνιάς, ἡ, Call.Cer.75, AP9.743 (Theodorid.): Ἰτωνίς, ἡ, A.R.1.551, AP6.130 (Leon.).

Ο ο, sixteenth (later fifteenth) letter in Gr. alphabet : as numeral οʹ = 70, but ͵ο = 70,000. The name of the letter was οὖ, Pl.Cra.414c, al., Callias ap.Ath.10.453d, Neoptol. ap. eund.10.454f, AP9.385 (Steph.Gramm.): this name was in Plato's time pronounced like the letter itself, Pl.Cra.393d; ο scanned long, Achae.33.4; τὸ ο̄ (v.l. ͞ου) στοιχεῖον Suid. s.v. Φιλοξένου γραμμάτιον; later called ὂ μικρόν (Hdn.Epim.209, Theognost.Can.13), little or short o, opp. ὢ μέγα great or long o.

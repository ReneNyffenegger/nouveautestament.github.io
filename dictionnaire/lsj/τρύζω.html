τρύζω, Hp.Mul.2.131, al., Arat.948, etc.: Ep. impf. τρύζεσκον Theoc.7.140: aor. ἔτρυξα Sopat.5 codd.Ath. (ἔγρυξεν Schw., Kaibel), (ἐπ-) Babr.112.8: mostly used in pres. and impf. :—make a low murmuring sound, of the note of the ὀλολυγών, Theoc. l. c., Arat. l. c., AP5.291 (Agath.); of the τρυγών, Poll.5.89, Eust.229.24, Ps.-Hdn. in Stud.Ital.1.80 (cf. τρυγόζω): metaph., of men, mutter, murmur, Il.9.311.     2. of liquids, squirt out with a noise, of diarrhoea, Hp.Prog.11; but τ. τὸ οὖρον Id.Mul.2.131, cf. Steril.247, Prorrh.2.4, seems to mean comes by drops.     3. creak, of shoes, only in Philostr.Ep.37 (dub.). (Onomatop.)

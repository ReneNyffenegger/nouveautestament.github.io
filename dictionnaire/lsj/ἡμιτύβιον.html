ἡμιτύβιον [ῡ], τό, linen cloth, towel, napkin, Sapph.116, Hp.Art.37, Ar.Pl.729; of a kind of material, Michel832.23 (Samos, iv B.C.). (Egypt. acc. to Poll.7.71:—in codd. sts. ἡμιτύμβιον, as in Aret.CD1.3, v.l. in Lib.Decl.26.42, which is interpr. by Suid., a half (i.e. small) grave; but prob. this form is due to the copyists, who wished to find a meaning in the word.)

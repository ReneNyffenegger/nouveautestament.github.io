κῡρι-ακός, ή, όν, (κύριος) of or for an owner or master, Stud.Pal.22.177.18 (ii A.D.); but usu. of the Roman Emperor, ὁ κ. φίσκος the fiscus, CIG2827 (Aphrod.), Supp.Epigr.2.567 (Caria (?)); κ. ψῆφοι, λόγος, OGI669.13, 18 (Egypt, i A.D.); κ. χρῆμα POxy.474.41 (ii A.D.).     II. esp. belonging to the Lord (Christ): Κ. δεῖπνον the Lord's Supper, 1 Ep.Cor.11.20; ἡ Κ. ἡμέρα the Lord's day, Apoc.1.10; τὸ Κυριακόν (sc. δῶμα) the Lord's house, Edict.Maximini ap.Eus.PE9.10.     III. Subst. κυριακός, ὁ, spirit invoked in magic, PMag.Par.1.916.

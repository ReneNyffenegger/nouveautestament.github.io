ἀγών-ιος (A), ον, of or belonging to the contest, ἄεθλος ἀ. its prize, Pi.I.5(4).7; εὖχος Id.O.10(11).63; πούς Simon.29:— epith. of Hermes as president of games, Pi.I.1.60, cf. IG5(1).658; of Zeus as decider of the contest, S.Tr.26:—ἀ. θεοί, in A.Ag.513, Supp.189,242, Pl.Lg.783a, either gods in assembly, or the gods who presided over the great games (Zeus, Poseidon, Apollo, and Hermes), = ἀγοραῖοι θ., Eust.1335.58.     2. ἀγωνίῳ σχολᾷ S.Aj.194, either pause from battle, or strenuous rest (oxymoron, cf. Sch.).

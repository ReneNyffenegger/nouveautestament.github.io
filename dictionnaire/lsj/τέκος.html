τέκος, εος, τό, Ep. dat. pl. τέκεσσι Il.5.71, al., τεκέεσσι 3.160, al.:—poet. for τέκνον, 18.63, 24.36, al., Hes.Sc.216, al.; as a term of endearment from elders to their youngers, φίλον τέκος Il.9.437, 444, etc.: also in Alc.Supp.10.7, Pi.I.6(5).30, B.6.13, al., A.Th.203,677, E.HF439, Hec.475 (mostly lyr.).     2. of animals, Il.8.248: esp. in pl. the young, 12.222, al., Ar.Pl.292.     3. metaph., δυσσεβίας μὲν ὕβρις τέκος A.Eu.534 (lyr.).

ἀτηρός [ᾱ], ά, όν, blinded by ἄτη, hurried to ruin, Thgn.433,634; φρήν S.Fr.264.     II. baneful, mischievous, δύη A.Pr.746; τύχη Id.Ag.1483 (lyr.); κακόν E.Andr.353; ναυτιλίη AP9.23 (Antip.); τὸ ἀ. bane, mischief, A.Eu.1007 (anap.); μή τι ἀ. ποιέωσι [οἱ παῖδες] Democr.279.—Once in Com., ἀτηρότατον κακόν an 'outrageous' nuisance, Ar.V.1299; and so Adv. ἀτηρ-ῶς 'awfully' as a slang word, Phld.Mus. p.105K.: in Pl.Cra.395b and c introduced only for an etym. purpose: also in later Prose, D.L.6.99.

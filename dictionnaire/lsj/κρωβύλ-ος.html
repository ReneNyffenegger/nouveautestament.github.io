κρωβύλ-ος (parox., v. Hdn.Gr.1.163), ὁ, roll or knot of hair on the crown of the head, worn at Athens, κρωβύλον ἀναδούμενοι Th.1.6, cf. Antiph.189, Sch.Ar.Nu.980.     2. nickname of the orator Hegesippus, Aeschin.3.118.     3. name of a πορνοβοσκός: prov., Κρωβύλου ζεῦγος 'a precious pair', Lib.Ep.91.2, Hsch., etc.     II. tuft of hair on a helmet, X.An.5.4.13.

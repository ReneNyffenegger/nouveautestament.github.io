οὐγγία or οὐγκία, ἡ, Lat. uncia, as adopted by the Sicil. Greeks, Arist.Fr.510, Gal.13.789, Alex.Aphr.in Top.210.7:—written ὀγκία in Epich.203, Sophr.151: hence Adj. οὐγκιαῖος, α, ον, of one uncia, prob. in SIG1042.23 (Sunium, ii/iii A. D.): οὐγκιασμός, ὁ, measurement by unciae, in pl., Just.Nov.107.1.

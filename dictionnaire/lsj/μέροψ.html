μέροψ, οπος, ὁ, poet. word, used only in pl. as epith. of men, derived by Gramm. from μείρομαι, ὄψ, dividing the voice, i.e. articulate (cf. Hsch., Sch.Il.1.250), μ. ἄνθρωποι Il. l.c., Hes.Op.109, etc.; μ. βροτοί 11.2.285; μερόπεσσι λαοῖς A.Supp.90 (lyr.): hence as Subst., = ἄνθρωποι, Musae.Fr.13 D., A.Ch.1018 (anap.), E.IT1263 (lyr.), A.R.4.536, Call.Fr.418, AP7.563 (Paul. Sil.); a usage satirized by Strato Com.,1.6 sq.     II. in sg. and pl., bee-eater, Merops apiaster, Arist.HA615b25, Plu.2.976d; cf. εἴροψ.

οὐλαί, Att. ὀλαί, αἱ, barley-corns, barley-groats, which were sprinkled on the head of the victim before the sacrifice (cf. Sch.Ar.Eq.1164), Od.3.441, cf. Hdt.1.132, Ar.Eq.1167, Pax948, 960, Thphr.Char.10.13 (ὀλ-, v.l. οὐλ-), SIG1024.18 (Myconos, iii/ii B. C.); οὐλαὶ κριθῶν Hdt.1.160. (The variation οὐλαί: ὀλαί points to orig. *ὀλϝαί, cf. ὀλβάχνιον: the Arc. form ὀλοαί IG5(2).514.15 (Lycosura, ii B. C.) prob. comes from a bye-form *ὀλοϝαί. Perh. cogn. with ἀλέω (A), ἔλυμος III, ὄλυρα.)

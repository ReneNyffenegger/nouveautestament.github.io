ἄπιος (B), η, ον, far away, far off, distant, τηλόθεν ἐξ ἀπίης γαίης Il.1.270, 3.49, Od.16.18, cf. S.OC1685.     II. Ἄπιος, α, ον, Apian, i.e. Peloponnesian, said (in this sense) to be derived from Ἆπις, Apis, a mythical king of Argos, A.Supp.260, cf. Paus.2.5.7; Ἀπία γῆ, Ἀπία χθών, or Ἀπία alone, the Peloponnese, esp. Argolis, A.Ag.256, S.OC1303, Ath.14.650b, etc.; cf. Ἀπίς prob. in A.R.4.1564. [The former word has ᾰ, the latter ᾱ; yet S.OC1685 uses signf. I with ᾱ, and later Ep. Poets have signf. II with ᾰ, cf. Rhian.13.] (Commonly derived from ἀπό, as ἀντίος from ἀντί; and Hsch. expl. ἐξ ἀπίης γαίης by ἀλλοτρίας ἢ ξένης ἢ μακρὰν οὔσης, cf. Str.8.6.9.)

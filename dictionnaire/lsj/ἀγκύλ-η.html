ἀγκύλ-η [ῠ], ἡ, (ἄγκος) properly, like ἀγκάλη, bend of the arm or wrist, ἀπ’ ἀγκύλης ἱέναι, a phrase descriptive of the way in which the cottabus was thrown, B.Fr.13.2, cf. Hsch.; ἀπ’ ἀγκύλης ἵησι λάταγας Cratin.273; wrongly expl. as cup, Ath.11.782d.     2. bend of the knee, ham, Philostr.Im.2.6, Sch.Il.23.726.     3. joint bent and stiffened by disease, Hp.Liqu.6, cf.Poll.4.196.     II. loop, noose, πλεκτὰς ἀγκύλας E.IT1408; in the leash of a hound, X.Cyn.6.1; in bandages, Gal.18(1).790; in torsion-engines, HeroBel.83.1.     2. thong of a javelin, by which it was hurled, Str.4.4.3; hence, the javelin itself, E.Or.1476, cf. Plu.Phil.6; δι’ ἀγκυλῶν ἱππόται Them.Or.21.256d.     3. bow-string, ἀ. χρυσόστροφοι S.OT203.     4. ἀγκύλη τῆς ἐμβάδος sandal-thong, Alex.31.     5. curtain-ring, hook, LXXEx.38.18(36.34), al.; hook for a door, IG11(2).165.11, al. (Delos, iii B.C.).     6. sides of the κεραία, Poll.1.91.

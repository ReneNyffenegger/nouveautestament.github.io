σίκινν-ῐς [σῐ], or σίκῑνις (E.Cyc.37), ιδος, ἡ, acc. Σίκιννιν D.H.7.72:—Sicinnis, a dance of Satyrs used in the Satyric drama, S.Fr.772, E. l.c., D.H. l.c., Luc.Salt.22: named from its inventor Sicinnus, Ath.1.20e, cf. Scamon1; or from Sicinnis, a nymph of Cybele, although originally danced in honour of Sabazios, Arr.Fr.106J.— Also written Σίκιννον, τό, Suid.; Σίκιννα, AB267.

E.Andr.267, etc.     II. plumbago, used as a test of gold, Thgn.417,1105,Arist.Mete.349a2.     2. circular piece of lead for drawing ruled lines, AP6.67 (Jul. Aeg.).     III. = μολύβδιον II, Hp.Mul.2.132,al.     2. μόλυβδος, ἡ, = μολυβδίς 3, Ammon.Diff.p.120 V.—Acc. to EM590.8, μόλυβδος and μόλιβος, with their respective derivs., are the correct forms, and this is corroborated by the earlier Inscrr. and Pap.; but later Inscrr., Pap., and codd. show much variation in spelling in derivs.; cf. βόλιμος, περιβολιβόω. (Prob. a foreign word like the cogn. Lat. plumbum.)

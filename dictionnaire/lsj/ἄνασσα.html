ἄνασσα (ϝάνασσα Inscr.Cypr.101 H., al.), ἡ, fem. of ἄναξ, queen, lady, addressed to goddesses, Od.3.380, 6.175; esp. in Att.to Athena, A.Eu.228,235,443, etc.     2. to a mortal, Od.6.149, etc.—Common in Poetry from Pi. downwds.; rare in Prose, as Isoc.9.72, Arist.Fr.526: c. dat., ἐνέροισιν A.R.3.862.     3. generally, like ἄναξ IV, ἄνασσα πράγους καὶ βουλεύματος authoress of this deed, E.Fr.704; ὀργίων Ar.Ra.387.     II. as Adj., royal, ἀ. βουλή, of the Roman Senate, IG14.1389i34.

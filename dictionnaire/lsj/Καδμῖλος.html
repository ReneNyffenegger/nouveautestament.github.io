Καδμῖλος (on the accent v. Hdn.Gr.1.162), ὁ, name of one of the Cabiri in Samothrace, St.Byz. s.v. Καβειρία, Hdn.Gr.2.446:— also Κασμ-ῖλος, Dionysodor. ap. Sch.A.R.1.917, cf. IG12(8).74 (Imbros, ii A.D.), Call.Fr.409; identified with Hermes, ibid., Hdn.Gr.1.162, Sch.Lyc.162: with Lat. camillus (casm-), Varr.LL7.3, cf. Plu.Num.7; Καδμῖλοι is prob. for Κάδωλοι, title of ministrants in the cult of the Curetes and Μεγάλοι Θεοί, D.H.2.22.

ἀοίδ-ιμος, ον, sung of, famous in song or story, Hdt.2.79,135, Pi.P.8.59, etc.; προφάταν Id.Pae.6.6; from Pi.(Fr.76) downwds. a favourite epith. of Athens; ἀ. πόμα a glorious draught, Id.N.3.79, etc.; ἀ. εὐνομίῃσιν famous for his justice, IG7.94 (Nisaea), cf. Luc.Tim.38, App.BC2.82, etc.: Sup., Plu.Ant.34:—only once in Hom., and in bad sense, notorious, infamous, ὡς . . ἀνθρώποισι πελώμεθ’ ἀοίδιμοι Il.6.358.     II. won by song, ἄγρα, of the Sphinx's victims, E.El.471 (lyr.).

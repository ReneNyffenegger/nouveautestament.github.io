ἀκρά̄-χολος [ρᾱ], ον, quick to anger irascible, Ar.Eq.41, Pl.R.411c, Phld.Lib.p.44 O., etc.; κύων ἀ. ill-tempered dog, Ar.Fr.594a; μέλισσα Epin.1.7: Sup., ἀχέρδου τῆς ἀκραχολωτάτης, of a spinous pear. Pherecr.164:—also ἀκρόχολος, ον, Arist.EN1126a18, Ph.2.268, Plu.2.604b, etc.     II. generally, in passionate distress, Theoc.24.61. (ἀκρᾱ- is confirmed by metre of Com., Ion. form ἀκρη-, and etym. (shortened fr. ἀκρᾱτ-); ἀκρό-χολος is freq. v.l. in codd. of early authors, as Pl. l. c.)

ζεῦγμα, ατος, τό, (ζεύγνυμι) that which is used for joining, band, bond, τὸ ζ. τοῦ λιμένος the barrier of ships moored across the mouth of the harbour, Th.7.69, cf. 70, D.S.13.14:—written ζεογμα, Rev.Phil.50.70 (Didyma, ii B.C.).     2. bridge of boats, AP9.147 (Antag.); τὰ ζ. τῶν ποταμῶν D.H.9.31, cf. Plu.2.174e, etc.; pier or platform formed by lashing several vessels together, Plb.3.46.2, Plu.Marc.14, 15.     3. canal-lock, PPetr.2 p.123, 3 p.210 (iii B.C.): metaph., ζεύγματ’ ἀνάγκης the bonds of necessity, E.IA443.     II. Gramm., zeugma, a figure of speech, wherein two subjects are used jointly with the same predicate, which strictly belongs only to one, Alex.Fig.2.17.

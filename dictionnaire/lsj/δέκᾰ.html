δέκᾰ, οἱ, αἱ, τά, indecl., ten, Il.2.372, Od.9.160, etc.; οἱ δ. the Ten, Isoc.18.6; ἡ τῶν δ. τυραννίς Arist.Ath.41.2; also οἱ δ. the Attic Orators, Philostr.VS2.1.14; τὰ δέκα [ἔτη] ἀφ’ ἥβης those who are ten years past 20 (the age of military service), X.HG3.4.23; δ. ἄνδρες, = Lat. decemviri, App.Hann.56: compds. (not in early writers, but usu. in Hellenistic Gr.)

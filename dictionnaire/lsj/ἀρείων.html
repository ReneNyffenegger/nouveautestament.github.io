ἀρείων [ᾰ], ον, gen. ονος, used as Comp. of ἀγαθός, cf. ἄριστος: —better, stouter, braver, in Hom. of all advantages of body, birth, and fortune, Il.1.260, al., cf. Hes.Op.207, Pi.N.7.101, A.Th.305 (lyr.), Ag.81 (lyr.):—rare in Prose, ἃ ὑμῖν ἄρειον μὴ γνῶναι Arist.Fr.44.     II. ἀρείονες, οἱ, a kind of snail or slug, Ael.NA10.5.

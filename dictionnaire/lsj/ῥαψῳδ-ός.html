ῥαψῳδ-ός, ὁ, reciter of Epic poems, sts. applied to the bard who recited his own poem, as to Hesiod, Nicocl. ap. Sch.Pi.N.2.2 (v. infr.); but usu., professional reciters, esp. of the poems of Homer, Hdt.5.67, Pl.Ion530c, etc.: also ῥ. κύων, ironically, of the Sphinx who chanted her riddle, S.OT391. (Prob. from ῥάπτω, ἀοιδή; Hes.Fr.265 speaks of himself and Homer as ἐν νεαροῖς ὕμνοις ῥάψαντες ἀοιδήν, and Pi.N.2.2 calls Epic poets ῥαπτῶν ἐπέων ἀοιδοί: not from ῥάβδος (cf. ῥάβδος I.6) as if ῥαβδῳδός (Eust.6.24, ῥαβδῳδία ib.16).)

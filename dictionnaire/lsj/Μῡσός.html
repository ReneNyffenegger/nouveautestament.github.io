Μῡσός, ὁ, Mysian, A.Pers.52 (anap.), etc.: proverbially feeble and effeminate, whence prov. Μυσῶν λεία, i. e. a prey to all, of anything that can be plundered with impunity, Stratt.35, D.18.72, Arist.Rh.1372b33; ὁ Μυσῶν ἔσχατος the most worthless of men, Magn.5, cf. Philem.77, Men.55; τῶν λεγομένων Μ. ὁ ἔσχατος Pl.Tht.209b; εἴ σοι Μυσόν γε ἥδιον καλεῖν Id.Grg.521b, cf. E.Fr.704.

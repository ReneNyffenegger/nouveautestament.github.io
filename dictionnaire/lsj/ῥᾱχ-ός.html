ῥᾱχ-ός, ἡ (in codd. freq. ῥάχος, but the Ion. form ῥηχός Hdt.7.142, and the compd. εὔρρηχος show that it must be either ῥᾱχός or ῥᾶχος:— in EM703.1 ῥάχος, ὁ):—thorn-hedge, S.Fr.812 (pl.), X.Cyn.10.7, cf. Poll.1.225; in Hdt. l.c., ῥηχός is prob. palisade or wattled fence; brushwood, ῥαχὸς (Dor. acc. pl.) καὶ φρύγανα GDI5027 (Crete).     2. generally, twig, branch, of the vine, Thphr.CP3.7.3; of the tamarisk, Hsch., Phot., dub. in Orph.Fr.31 ii 10.     II. at Troezen, wild olive tree, Paus.2.32.10.

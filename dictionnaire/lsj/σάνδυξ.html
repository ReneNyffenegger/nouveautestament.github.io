σάνδυξ, υκος, ἡ, a bright red colour, also called ἀρμένιον, Str.11.14.9 (prob. cj.); obtained by heating ψιμύθιον ( = cerussa), Dsc.5.88, cf. Plin.HN35.40; though a like colour was made from a plant of the same name, red sandalwood, Pterocarpus santalinus, Sosib.21, Verg.Ecl.4.45, Plin. l.c., Lyd.Mag.3.64.     2. pl., flesh-coloured women's garments dyed with this colour, in Lydia, ibid.     3. a kind of salve, prob. a pink mixture of zinc oxide and carbonate, Dsc. l.c., Gal.12.244, Hsch.     II. casket, Id. [ū in genit., Prop.2.19.81; but ǔ in Grattius Cyn.86.] (Assyr. sâmtu, sându 'red stone', prob. cinnabar.)

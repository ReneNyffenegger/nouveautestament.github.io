σαμβύκ-η [ῡ], ἡ, a triangular musical instrument with four strings, Arist.Pol.1341b1, Neanth.5 J., Juba 73; of barbaric origin, Str.10.3.17. (Aramaic sabbekhā (perh. not Semitic), with m inserted, as in Ἀμβακούμ = Habakkuk. etc.).     2. = σαμβυκίστρια, Plb.5.37.10; with pun on signf. II, Id.8.6.6.     II. an engine of like form used in sieges, Id.8.4.8, al., Bito57.1, Plu.Marc.15, Ath.Mech.27.7, App.Mith.26.—Cf. σάμβυξ. [Penult. long in sambūca, Pers.5.95.]

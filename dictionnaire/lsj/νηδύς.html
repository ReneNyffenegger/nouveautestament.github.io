νηδύς, ύος, ἡ, any of the cavities in the body, Hp.de Arte10: hence,     1. stomach, Od.9.296, Hes.Th.487, S.OC1263, etc.     2. belly, paunch, Il.13.290, Hdt.2.47.     3. bowels, A.Ch.757, Hp.Aër.19, etc.; ἐξελεῖν τὴν νηδύν Hdt.2.87; as the seat of thirst, τέγξας ἄδιψον νηδύν E.Cyc.574; ἄρδῃ τε νηδύν Id.Supp.207.     4. womb, Il.24.496, Hes.Th.460, A.Eu.665, etc.; also of Zeus when in travail of Athena, Hes.Th.890,899; of Dionysus, E.Ba.527 (lyr.).     5. metaph., ν. νάρθηκος Nic.Al.272; λέβητος Orph.L.276.—Acc. νηδύα for νηδύν in Q.S.1.616; dat. pl. νηδύσι Nic.Th.467.

κίρκος, ὁ, a kind of hawk or falcon, ἴρηξ κίρκος (where ἴρηξ is the generic term, κίρκος the specific), Od.13.87, cf. Il.22.139, A.Pr.857, Arist.HA620a18, Opp.C.1.64; κίρκου λεπάργου A.Fr.304.5. (The species cannot be identified.)     II. a kind of wolf, Opp.C.3.304.     III. circle, mostly in form κρίκος (q.v.): hence, ring, IG11 (2).161 B49 (Delos, iii B.C.): poet. for Prose κρίκος acc. to Poll.1.94:—neut. pl. κίρκα ἢ καταδέσματα PMag.Lond.121.299.     IV. later, = Lat.circus, Plb.30.22.2, Arr.Epict.3.16.14, Plu.Aem.32.     V. unknown stone, Plin.HN37.153.     VI. = κωπηλάτης, Hsch., Phot.     VII. = ἡ τοῦ αἰγείρου βλάστησις, Hsch.

ὄρχᾰμος, ὁ, leader, chief, early Ep. only in the phrases ὄρχαμος ἀνδρῶν Il.2.837, al., Hes.Fr.96.12, ὄρχαμε λαῶν Il.14.102, al.; the former applied to the swineherd Eumaeus, Od.14.22, 121, and the cowherd Philoetius, 20.185,254.—Ep. word, used once by A., ὄ. στρατοῦ Pers.129 (lyr.); ὄρχαμε τ[όξου̣] IG12.516: in late Poetry without a gen., AP11.284 (Pall.).

ὅδ-ιος, ον, (ὁδός) belonging to a way or journey, ὄρνις ὅ. a bird of omen for the journey (or seen by the way), A.Ag.157 (lyr.); ὅ. κράτος αἴσιον ib.104 (lyr.); Ἑρμῆς ὅ. H. the guardian of roads and travellers, whose statues stood on the road-side, Hsch.     II. ὅδιον, τό, travelling expenses, prob. in Inscr.Magn.52.39.

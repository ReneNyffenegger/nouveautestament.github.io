ῥίον [ῐ], τό, any jutting part of a mountain, whether upwards or forwards; hence,     1. peak, περὶ ῥίον Οὐλύμποιο Il.8.25, cf. 14.154, 225, etc.; ῥ. ὀρέων Od.9.191; ῥ. οὔρεος h.Ap.139.     2. headland, Od.3.295; hence as pr. name of several places, esp. Ῥ. Μολυκρικόν and Ῥ. Ἀχαϊκόν at the mouth of the gulf of Corinth, Th.2.86 (cf. 84).     3. later, bay formed by a foreland, Ael.NA15.3. (Perh. akin to ῥίς, cf. Ness, Naze with nose.)

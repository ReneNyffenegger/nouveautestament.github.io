Λοκρ-οί, οἱ, the Locrians, of whom there were three tribes, the Opuntian, opposite Euboea, Il.2.527, Th.1.108, Str.9.3.1,9.3.17; the Epicnemidian, on Mt. Cnemis on the Maliac Gulf, Id.ll.cc.; and the Ozolian, on the Corinthian Gulf, Th.1.5, 103, etc.: the Epizephyrian or Zephyrian were a colony of the last on Mt. Zephyrium in lower Italy, Pi.O.10(11).13, Th.4.24 sq.,7.1, etc.: prov. Λοκρῶν σύνθημα, of deceit, Eust.275.43, Hsch., Suid.:—Adj. Λοκρός, ά, όν, Locrian, Lyc.1429:—also Λοκρικός, ή, όν, Poll.4.65, etc.:—fem. Λοκρίς, ίδος, Pi.P.2.19; ἡ Λ. (sc. γῆ) Ar.Av.152, etc.

σῐγύνης [ῡ], ου, ὁ, spear, Hdt.5.9, Opp.C.1.152; also σίγῡνος, ὁ, A.R.2.99, AP6.176 codd. (Maced.); σίγῡνον, τό, Arist.Po.1457b6, AP7.578 (Agath.); and in Lyc.556, σίγυμνον (in dat. -ῳ).—Cyprian acc. to Hdt. and Arist. ll. cc., Scythian acc. to Sch.Par.A.R.4.320 (cf. 111).     II. σιγύνης among the Ligyes near Marseilles was used for κάπηλος, Hdt. l.c.     III. Σιγύνναι, οἱ, a people on the Middle Danube, Hdt. l.c.; in A.R.4.320, Σίγυννοι; in Str.11.11.8, Σίγιννοι. [In A.R. and Opp., ῡ; freq. written with double ν, σιγύννης, etc.]

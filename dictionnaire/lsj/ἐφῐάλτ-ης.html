ἐφῐάλτ-ης, ου, ὁ, Aeol. ἐπιάλτης Alc.129, also ἐπίαλος ibid.:—nightmare, conceived as a throttling demon, Phryn.Com.1, Dsc.3.140 (pl.), Artem.2.37, Ruf. ap. Orib.7.26.177, Str.1.2.8; pr. n. of one of the Aloidae, Il.5.385, Od.11.308, Pi.P.4.89 (Ἐπ-ῐάλτης acc. to Sch.Od. l.c., cf. A.D.Synt.179.22), and Att. pr. n., cf. Ael.Dion.Fr.381, IG12.950.92, etc. (Identified with ἠπιόλης by A.D.Fr.8.12 (or Apollodorus, v. Sophr.68 note); ἐπιάλτης is expld. as ῥιγοπύρετον by Suid.; popularly connected with ἐφάλλομαι, Sch.BIl.5.385, or ἐφίαλλω, EM403.32; cf. incubo.)

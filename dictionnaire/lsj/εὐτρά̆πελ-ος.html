εὐτρά̆πελ-ος, ον, (τρέπω) easily turning or changing, of the Athenians, Ael.VH5.13; nimble, of apes, Id.NA5.26; in earlier Gr. always metaph., λόγος εὐ. a dexterous, ready plea, Ar.V.469 (lyr.). Adv. εὐτρᾰπέ-λως dexterously, readily, without awkwardness, Th.2.41.     2. of persons, ready with an answer or repartee, witty, Arist.EN1108a24, 1128a10; εὐ. παρὰ τὰς συνουσίας Plb.23.5.7; τίτθη εὐ. Jul.Or.7.227a: Sup., Plb.9.23.3.     b. in bad sense, jesting, ribald, Isoc.7.49; εὐτράπελόν ἐστι c. acc. et inf., it is ludicrous that . . , Plu.2.1062b.     3. tricky, dishonest, v.l. in Pi.P.4.105; εὐ. κέρδη time-serving arts, of flatterers, ib.1.92.

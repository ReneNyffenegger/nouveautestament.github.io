αἱμό-ρροος, ον, contr. αἱμό-ρρους, ουν, flowing with blood, τρώματα Hp.Art.69; αἱ. φλέβες veins so large as to cause a haemorrhage if wounded, Id.Fract.11, ubi v.Gal.     2. suffering from haemorrhoids, Hp.Epid.4.7.     II. as Subst., a serpent, whose bite makes blood flow from all parts of the body, Philum.Ven.21, Nic.Th.282; cf. αἱμορροΐς III.

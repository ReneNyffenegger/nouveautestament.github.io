ὑπωμοσία, ἡ, oath taken in court to delay proceedings (v. ὑπόμνυμι II),     1. oath or affidavit showing good ground for the absence of a party to a suit, application for delay, D.21.84 (pl.), v. Harp. s. v.; ὑπωμοσίαν παραδέχεσθαι Hyp.Eux.7; cf. ἀνθυπόμνυμι.     2. oath taken by the prosecutor in a γραφὴ παρανόμων (v. παράνομος 11.2), with the effect of suspending the proposed law or decree, ἐᾶν [τὸν νόμον] ἐν ὑ. to leave it in the condition caused by the ὑπωμοσία, to let it drop, D.18.103; cf. AB313, Poll.8.56.

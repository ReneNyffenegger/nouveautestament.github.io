Λάρῑσα [ᾱρ], ἡ (not Λάρισσα, v. Arc.77.14, IG9(2).60.5, 525.5, al., but Λαρισσέοις ib.9(2).6c3), Larissa, a name of many old Greek cities, Il.2.841, etc.; Pelasgic acc. to Str.9.5.19, 13.3.2: an Ion. form Λήρισαι (in Aeolis) occurs in Hdt.1.149; orig. it denoted a citadel, such as the Larissa of Argos, St.Byz., Sch.A.R.1.40.     II. Adj. Λᾱρῑσαῖος, α, ον, Larissaean, of or from Larissa, Th.2.22, X.HG3.1.7, etc.; Ion. Ληρισ-αῖος Hdt.9.1 and 58:—also Λαρίσιος and Λαρισηνός as epith. of Zeus, Str.9.5.19, 13.3.2.     2. Λᾱρῑσαῖοι ἑψητῆρες Larissaean pots for boiling, AP6.305 (Leon.); λᾱρῑσοποιοί for λαρισαιοποιοί, either makers of Larissaean pots, or makers of Larissaean citizens, of the δημιουργοί (magistrates), Gorg. ap. Arist.Pol.1275b30.

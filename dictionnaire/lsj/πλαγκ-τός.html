πλαγκ-τός, ή, όν, also ός, όν A.Ag.593: (πλάζω A):—poet. Adj. wandering, roaming, of ships, Id.Pers.277 (lyr.); πλαγκτὰ δ’ ὡσεί τις νεφέλα E.Supp.961 (lyr.); π. ὕδωρ, of the Euripús, AP9.73 (Antiphil.); ἰός ib.6.75 (Paul. Sil.); πλαγκτὰν ὁδόν a devious route, Hymn.Is.149.     b. π. ἄστρὰ, = πλάνητες, Alex.Eph. ap. Theo Sm.p.140 H.     2. metaph., wandering in mind, erring, distraught, Od.21.363, A.Ag.593.     II. Πλαγκταὶ πέτραι rocks near Scylla and Charybdis, Od.12.59 sqq., 23.327; later identified with the Συμπληγάδες or Κυάνεαι of the Bosporus, Hdt.4.85, Arr.Peripl.M.Eux.25, Eratosth. ap. Sch.E.Med.2, etc.; but also with the volcanic islands of Lipari, A.R.4.924, cf. Apollod.1.9.25.

Τρῐκάρᾱνος [κᾰ], ὁ, the Three-headed, a satirical attack on three cities, Sparta, Athens, and Thebes, written by Anaximenes of Lampsacus (Frr.20 and 21 J.) and ascribed by him to Theopompus, Paus.6.18.5, Luc.Pseudol.29, Fug.32; also a satire by Varro on the coalition of Caesar, Pompeius, and Crassus, App.BC2.9.

κουρ-εῶτις (sc. ἡμέρα, Hsch.; κ. ἑορτή Alciphr.3.46), ιδος, ἡ, the third day of the Ἀπατούρια, on which children were presented to the φράτερες, and boys competed in recitation, Pl.Ti.21b; ἡ κ. Ἀπατουρίων IG22.1237.28. (Perh. from κείρω, κουρά, because the child's hair is said to have been cut on that day, Hsch.)

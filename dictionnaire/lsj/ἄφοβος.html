ἄφοβος, ον, without fear, and so:     1. fearless, intrepid, Pi.I.5(4).40, S.OC1325, etc.; πρὸς ἐρημίαν, περὶ τοῦ μέλλοντος, Plu.Lyc.16, Galb.23: c. gen., having no fear of, τῶν ἀρχόντων D.Chr.2.52; τὸ ἄφοβον, = ἀφοβία, Pl.La.197b. Adv. ἀφό-βως X.Hier.7.10, Pl.Lg.682c, PTeb.24.74 (ii B. C.).     2. causing no fear, free from fear, A.Pr.902; λόγος οὐκ ἄ. εἰπεῖν Pl.Lg.797a.     3. ἄ. θῆρες, in S.Aj.366, is an oxymoron, beasts which fear not men or which no one fears, tame beasts, cattle.

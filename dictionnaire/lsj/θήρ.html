Ἔρωτος) Alex.245.12: prov., ἔγνω θὴρ θῆρα Arist.Rh.1371b16.     2. of any living creature, πλωτοὶ θῆρες, i.e. dolphins, Arion 1.5; of vermin killed by birds, Ar.Av.1064 (lyr.); of gnats, AP5.150 (Mel.); of the sacred animals in Egypt, ἀρχιστολιστὴς θηρῶν Sammelb.4011.4.     3. any fabulous monster, as the Sphinx, A.Th.558 codd.; esp. of a centaur, S.Tr.556, 568 (cf. φήρ); of Satyrs, E.Cyc.624; οὐ θεῶν τις οὐδ’ ἄνθρωπος οὐδὲ θ.

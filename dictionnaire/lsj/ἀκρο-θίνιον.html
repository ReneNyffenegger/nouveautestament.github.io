ἀκρο-θίνιον [θῑ], τό, E.Ph.282, Th.1.132, Pl.Lg.946b; mostly pl. ἀκρο-θίνια or ἀκρό-θῑνα, Pi.N.7.41, al.: sg.ἀκρό-θις, ἡ, acc. -θινα GDI2561 D 47 Rüsch (Delph., iv B. C.): (ἄκρος, θίς):—topmost or best part of heap; hence, first fruits of the field, booty, etc., offered to the gods, Simon.109, Hdt.1.86, 90, al., Pi.l.c., etc.; ἀ. τῆς Μαραθῶνι μάχης Michel1117 (Delph.); ἀκρόθινα πολέμου, in Pi.O.2.4, of the Olympic games, as founded from spoils taken in war, cf. ib.10(11).57.—Properly neut. Adj., A.Eu.834 ἀκροθίνια θύη offerings of first fruits. Post-Hom., rare in early Prose.

κόθορνος, ὁ, buskin, high boot, Hdt.1.155, 6.125, Ar.Lys.657, Lysipp.2, etc.; worn by tragic actors in heroic characters: hence,     2. emblem of Tragedy in the person of Dionysus, Ar.Ra.47,557.     3. since the buskins might be worn on either foot, ὁ Κόθορνος, nickname for a trimmer or timeserver, such as Theramenes, X.HG2.3.31: prov., εὐμεταβολώτερος κοθόρνου Zen.3.93, etc.

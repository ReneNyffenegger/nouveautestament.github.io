Δῐῑπετής (better Διειπετής, cf. Zenodor. ap. Sch.Od.4.477), ές, (πίπτω) fallen from Zeus, i. e. from heaven, Ep. epith. of streams, fed or swollen by rain, Il.16.174, Od.4.477, Hes.Fr.217; νάματ’ οὐ δ., of stagnant water, E.Hyps.Fr.5(3).31; δ. ὕδατα, of rain, Plu.Mar.21.     2. generally, divine, bright, χαλκός Emp.100.9; αἰθὴρ διϊπετής divine, holy, E.Ba.1267; δ. πυρσοῖς gleaming with fires, Id.Rh.43 (lyr.).     3. in continual flow, Hp.Mul.1.24 (expld.as = διαυγής, καθαρός, Erot.).     4. διϊπετέες οἰωνοί, prob. hovering in the sky, h.Ven.4.

ἀκρό-ᾱμα, ατος, τό, anything heard, esp. with pleasure, piece read, recited, played or sung, X.Smp.2.2, Hier.1.14; ἀ. καὶ ὁράματα Arist.EN1173b18; ἀ. καὶ πότοι Plb.31.25.4.     II. pl. for concrete, lecturers, singers, or players, esp. during meals, Phylarch.62, BCH30.272 (Delph.), Plb.4.20.10, 16.21.12; so in Lat., acroama Cic.Sest.54.116, etc.

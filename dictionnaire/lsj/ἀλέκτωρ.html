ἀλέκτωρ (A), [ᾰ], ορος, ὁ, poet. form of ἀλεκτρυών, cock, ἕως ἐβόησεν ἀ. Batr.192, cf. Pi.O.12.14, Simon.80 B, A.Ag.1671, Eu.861, Herod.4.12, etc.; later Prose, Arist.Fr.347, PTeb.140 (i B. C.)LXXPr.24.66 (30.31), Ev.Matt.26.34,al., IG3.77: metaph., of a trumpeter, κοινὸς Ἀθηναίων ἀ. Demad.Fr.4; of a flute, Ion Trag.39.     2. ἀλέκτορος λόφος yellow rattle, Rhinanthus major, Plin.HN27.40.     II. husband, consort, Tz.ad Lyc.1094, and so perh. in B.4.8, S.Fr.851. (Perh., like ἀκοίτης, ἄλοχος, from ἀ- copul., λέκτρον).

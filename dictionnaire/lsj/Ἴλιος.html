Ἴλιος [ῑλ], ου, ἡ, Ilios or Ilium, the city of Ilus, Troy, Il.5.210, al., Alc. Supp.8.4, E.Andr.103 (eleg.): Ἴλιον, τό, Hom. only in Il.15.71, but always in Trag. (exc. E.l.c.):—hence Ep. genitives, Ἰλιόθεν, from Troy, Il.14.251, Od.9.39; Ἰλιόθι πρό before Troy, 8.581, etc.; Ἰλιόφι κλυτὰ τείχεα the walls of Troy, Il.21.295.     II. as Adj., Ἴλιος, α, ον, Ilian, Trojan, Ἀθάνα E.Hec.1008: Ἴλι-ος, ον is f.l. in Id.Hel.1164 (lyr.).

δηλονότι, i.e. δῆλόν [ἐστιν] ὅτι (cf. δῆλος II.3), used adverbially, clearly, manifestly, Pl.Cri.53a, Grg.487d, etc.; once in NT, 1 Ep.Cor.15.27.     II. freq. epexegetically, that is to say, namely, Pl.Smp.199a, X.Cyr.5.4.6, etc.: in Gramm. the common form for introducing an explanation, Sch.Ar.Ach.11, etc.

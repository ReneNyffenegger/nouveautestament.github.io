πᾰν-δέκτης, ου, ὁ, all-receiver: in pl. πανδέκται, οἱ, name of a Universal Dictionary or Encyclopedia, such as those compiled by Tiro and Dorotheus, Gell.13.9.     2. in pl., also, the Pandects, i.e. the law-books of Justinian, Cod.Just.1.17.1.12, al.: sg., ὁ π., = Digesta, Id.Const. Δέδωκεν I.     II. Stoic word for ἐπίρρημα II, Charis.p.190 K.

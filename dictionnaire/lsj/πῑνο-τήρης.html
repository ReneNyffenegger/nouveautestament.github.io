πῑνο-τήρης, ου, ὁ, (τηρέω) pinna-guard, a small crab that lives in the pinna's shell, said to give warning of approaching danger, Arist.HA547b28, Chrysipp.Stoic.2.207, Plu.2.980b; cf. πινυφύλαξ.     2. metaph., of a diminutive person, 'baby-crab', Ar.V.1510; ὁ π. τοῦδε μάντεως χορός (prob. for χοροῦ), posted to give warning, S.Fr.113.

ὄρχις, ιος and εως, ὁ, Att. nom. pl. ὄρχεις, Ion. ὄρχιες, testicle, freq. in pl., testicles, Hdt.4.109, Hp.Aër.4, Eub.63.4 (anap.), etc.; cf. ὄσχις.     2. in females, ovaries, Gal.2.810, al.     II. plant so called from the form of its root, salep, Orchis papilionacea, and O. longicruris, Thphr.HP9.18.3, Dsc.3.126.     III. ὄρχις, ἡ, a kind of olive, Colum.5.8; cf. ὀρχάς (B). (Cf. Avest. arazi 'testicles'.)

ἐρῑν-άζω, aor. I inf. ἐρινάξαι and ἐρινάσαι, Hsch.:—hang fruiting branches of the wild fig (ἐρινεός) near the cultivated fig (συκῆ) in order that the gall-insect (ψήν) which lives in the wild fruit may carry pollen to the σῦκον, Thphr.CP2.9.5:—Pass., τὸ ἐρινασμένον the fig subjected to caprification, Id.HP2.8.3.     II. gather wild figs, Poll.7.143.

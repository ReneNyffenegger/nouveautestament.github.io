γλαύξ, Att. γλαῦξ, γλαυκός, ἡ, Euphronius ap. Sch.Ar.V.1081, cf. Hdn.Gr.2.947:—the little owl, Athene noctua, so called from its glaring eyes, Epich.166, Arist.HA488a26, al.; freq. as emblem of Athena, Ar.Av.516, Eq.1093, etc.: prov., γλαῦκ’ Ἀθήναζε, γλαῦκ’ εἰς Ἀθήνας, 'carry coals to Newcastle', Ar.Av.301, Antiph.175.2; γλαῦξ ἐν πόλει 'Jack's as good as his master', Hsch., etc.; γλαῦκες Λαυρειωτικαί, of Athenian coins, from the type, Ar.Av.1106; so of figures of owls, IG2.678B76.     2. γ. θαλαττία, an unknown bird, Thphr.Sign.52.     II. a kind of dance, Ath.14.629f.     III. wart cress, Coronopus procumbens, Dsc.4.138, Gal.11.857:—also γλάξ, Hdn.Gr.1.395, al.

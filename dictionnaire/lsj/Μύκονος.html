Μύκονος [ῠ], ἡ, Myconus, one of the Cyclades, Hdt.6.118, etc.; the people were said to be all bald, Lucil.1211, Str.10.5.9: whence (acc. to Donat.ad Ter.Hec.440) the proverb μία Μύκονος 'it's all one, all alike', Plu.2.616b, Them.Or.21.250c, Zen.5.17 (with a different expl.), prob. cj. in Luc.DMort.1.3; πάνθ’ ὑπὸ μίαν Μύκονον Str.l.c. (with a third expl., cf. Eust.ad D.P.525): Adj. Μῠκόνιος, α, ον, prov. Μ. γείτων, of a bad neighbour, Zen.5.21 (Μηκώνιος codd., Μυκώνιος Phot., Suid.), cf. Cratin.328, Com.Adesp.439; also ἡ λεγομένη Μυκόνιος ἀγροικία Jul.Mis.349d.

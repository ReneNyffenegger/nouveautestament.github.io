μῶλῠ, τό, moly, a fabulous herb, Od.10.305, cf. Com.Adesp.641; cf. μῶλυς II.     II. in later writers, garlic, Allium nigrum, Thphr.HP9.15.7, Dsc.3.47; cf. μώλυζα.     2. a kind of πήγανον ἄγριον growing in Cappadocia, ib.46.     3. = ἠρύγγιον, Ps.-Dsc.3.21.     4. = στρύχνον ὑπνωτικόν, Plin.HN21.180. (Cf. Skt.mū´lam 'root', mūlakarma 'magical use of roots'.)

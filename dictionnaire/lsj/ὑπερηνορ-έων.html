ὑπερηνορ-έων, οντος, ὁ, exceedingly manly: but always used in bad sense (though ἠνορέη is = ἀνδρεία, manliness, courage), overbearing, overweening, of the Trojans, Il.4.176; of Deïphobus (the Trojan), 13.258; of the Cyclopes, Od.6.5; in Od. mostly of the suitors, 17.482, al.; κακῶς ὑπερηνορέοντες 2.266, cf. 4.766.     II. Com., thinking oneself more than man, Ar.Pax53. (No Verb ὑπερηνορέω occurs: cf. ὑπερμενέων.)

Τίτῠρος [ῑ], ὁ, = Σάτυρος, Ael.VH3.40; Dor. acc. to Eust.1157.39; Sicilian for Σιληνοί or Σάτυροι or τράγοι acc. to Sch.Theoc.3.2 (dub. l.); but Str. distinguishes Τίτυροι from Σάτυροι and Σιληνοί, 10.3.15.     2. a common shepherd's name, Theoc.3.2.     II. τίτυρος, ὁ,     1. = σάτυρος I.3, short-tailed ape, Thphr.Char.5.9, Sch. Theoc.l.c.     2. Lacon. name for the bell-wether, Serv.ad Verg.E. Prooem.; he-goat, Sch.Theoc.l.c.; also called τιτυρίς, Phot.     3. a kind of bird, also τιτύρας, Hsch.; cf. τατύρας.     4. reed or pipe (cf. τιτύρινος), Id.

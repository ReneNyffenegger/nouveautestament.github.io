Τρῑτων-ίς, ίδος, ἡ, Tritonis, a lake in Libya famous in old Greek legends, Pi.P.4.20, Hdt.4.178; cf. Τριτογένεια.     2. a spring in Arcadia, also connected by legends with the birth of Athena, Paus.8.26.6.     II. a name of Athena (cf. Τριτογένεια), A.R.1.109.     III. vase in the form of a Triton, PPetr.2p.54, prob. in 3p.113 (iii B. C.).

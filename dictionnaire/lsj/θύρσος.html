θύρσος, ὁ, in late Poets with heterocl. pl. θύρσα AP6.158 (Sabin.): —wand wreathed in ivy and vine- leaves with a pine-cone at the top, carried by the devotees of Dionysus, E.Ba.80 (lyr.), SIG1109.138, HeroSpir.2.9, etc.; also of the devotees themselves, Sch.E.Hec.261.     II. = κλάδος, ῥάβδος, Hsch. (Prob. a loan-word.)

OC1637: Comp., ὁρκιωτέραν δ’ ἤμην τὰν δώλαν the slave's oath shall carry the greater weight, Leg.Gort.2.15.     2. that which is sworn by, ὅ. θεοί the gods invoked at an oath, who watch over its fulfilment and punish its violation, E.Ph.481, cf. IT747: in Prose, θεοὶ οἱ ὅ. Th.1.71,78; οἱ ὅ. θ. Aeschin.1.114; esp. Ζεὺς ὅ. S.Ph.1324, E.Hipp.1025, Arist.Mir.845b33, Paus.5.24.9sq., etc.; ὁρκία Θέμις E.Med. l.c.; φθιμένων σέβας ὅ. AP7.351 (Diosc.); ξίφος ὅ. a sword sworn by, E.Ph.1677.

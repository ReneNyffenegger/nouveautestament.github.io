ἀκερσεκόμης (ἀκερσι-κόμης in Nonn. ll. cc. infr.), ου, ὁ, (Dor. voc. -κόμα Pi.Pae.9.45: dat. pl. -κόμοισιν Nonn.D.14.232), (κείρω, κόμη) with unshorn hair, i.e. ever-young (for Greek youths wore long hair till they reached manhood), of Phoebus, Il.20.39, h.Ap.134, etc.     2. long-haired, Nonn.D.10.29, al.; cf. ἀκειρεκόμης.

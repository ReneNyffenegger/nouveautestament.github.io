φελλάτᾱς [λᾱ] λίθος, gen. α Polem.Hist.73, ὁ:—a kind of stone, of which statues were made, Polem.Hist. l.c., Hsch.; also φελλεάτας, called Dor. and expld. by κισσηρώδεις λίθοι in Sch.Ar.Nu.71 (φελλέτας, Suid.); prob. the same as pilates and pelastes cited (acc. to codd.) fr. Cato by Fest.p.268L. (Cf. sq.)

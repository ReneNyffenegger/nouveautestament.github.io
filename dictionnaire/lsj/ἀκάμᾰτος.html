ἀκάμᾰτος, ον, also η, ον Hes.Th.747, B.5.25, S.Ant.339:—without sense of toil, hence,     1. untiring, unresting, in Hom. always epith. of fire, Il.5.4, Od.20.123,al.; ἄνεμοι Emp.111.3; σθένος A.Pers.901; ἅλς B.l.c.; ἀ. γῆ earth that never rests from tillage, or inexhaustible, S.l.c.:—neut. ἀκάματα as Adv., Id.El.164(lyr.).     2. not tired, χείρ Hp.Fract.3; ὄμματα B.18.20.     3. metaph., δόξα 12.178; πρόνοια Stoic.1.125.     II. Act., not tiring, Aret.CD2.13. Adv. ἀκαμά̆-τως, in Comp., less painfully, Hp.Mul.1.1:—also ἀκαμᾰ-τεί Hsch. s.v. ἀκμητί. [ᾰκᾰμᾰτος S.El.164; but first syll. long in dactylic verse.]

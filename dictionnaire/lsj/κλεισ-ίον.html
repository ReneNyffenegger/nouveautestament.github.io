κλεισ-ίον, τό, outhouse, shed, τῆς οἰκίας τὸ κ. Antiph.21, cf. Lys.12.18, D.18.129 (here perh. = brothel), IG11(2).158 A56, 287 A146 (Delos, iii B.C.), BCH35.243 (ibid., ii B.C.), Ephes. 2.75 (i B.C.): pl., sheds for cattle, D.Chr.40.9.     2. shrine, chapel, Paus.4.1.7, BCH33.72 (Cappadocia). [First syll. long in Antiph. l.c.; written κλεισίον IG l.c., BCH35 l.c., Hdn.Gr.1.356, 2.415, Ael.Dion. l.c.; later κλισ-ίον Ephes.l.c., BCH33 l.c., freq. in codd.; prob. fr. κλίνω as 'lean-to', 'penthouse', rather than fr. κλείω as stated by Poll.9.50.]
